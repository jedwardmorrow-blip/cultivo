## Executive Summary

## 1. Revenue Share Mechanics

### 1.1 Revenue Definition

**"Partnership Revenue"** = gross software licensing and implementation services revenue attributable to Arroya customer accounts.

- **Scope:** SaaS fees ($4K–$10K/month), professional services ($25K avg), and support upsells
- **Exclusions:**
  - Beta/pilot accounts (first 60 days)
  - Internal testing and training accounts
  - Any revenue from non-Arroya substrate applications
  - Customer discounts or volume rebates (revenue net of standard discounts, gross of special negotiated terms)

### 1.2 Referral Fee Structure

**OPTION A: Tiered Revenue Share (Recommended)**

| Cumulative Annual Revenue (Year 1) | Revenue Share % |
|-----|---|
| $0–$250K | 15% |
| $250K–$500K | 12% |
| >$500K | 10% |

- **Rationale:** Incentivizes high-volume customer acquisition while protecting unit economics as volume increases
- **Calculation:** Applied monthly to incremental partnership revenue; reset annually Jan 1

**OPTION B: Fixed 12% Revenue Share (Conservative)**

- Flat 12% referral fee on all partnership revenue
- **Rationale:** Simpler accounting; predictable Arroya cost structure
- **Candidate if:** Arroya pushes for simplicity; we anticipate lower volume

**OPTION C: Fixed 10% + Performance Bonus (Aggressive Growth)**

- Base 10% referral fee
- Additional 2% bonus if annual revenue exceeds $500K
- Bonus paid as Q1 following year true-up
- **Rationale:** Aggressive growth incentive; aligns with land-and-expand model

**Recommendation:** OPTION A (tiered). Balances growth incentives with unit economics.

### 1.3 Calculation and Reporting

**Monthly Calculation:**
1. Calculate total partnership revenue for the calendar month
2. Apply applicable share tier (based on year-to-date cumulative)
3. Arroya fee = revenue × share percentage
4. Record in billing system with line-item detail (customer, product, amount)

**Monthly Invoice:**
- Due by 15th of following month
- Itemized by customer account
- Include prior-month reconciliation if any corrections identified
- Net 30 payment terms (see Section 2.2)

**Quarterly Business Review (QBR):**
- Scheduled monthly, min 15 min call with CRO + Arroya revenue lead
- Review customer pipeline, churn, expansion, payment delays
- Discuss marketing/go-to-market performance vs. targets (see Section 3)

## 2. Payment Schedule and Terms

### 2.1 Payment Frequency and Timing

| Payment Type | Due Date | Terms | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Monthly referral fee | 15th of following month | Net 30 | Invoiced via standard billing system |
| MDF reimbursement (if applicable) | As requested, within 30 days | Per-invoice approval | Requires advance Arroya approval |
| Bonus/true-up | 15th of Feb (following year) | Net 15 | Reconciliation statement included |

**Payment Method:**
- ACH bank transfer (standard)
- Wire transfer available on request (Arroya bears fees)
- Invoiced to Arroya's accounting; payable to CULT Inc.

### 2.2 Settlement and Cash Flow Impact

**Baseline (Year 1 Base Case):**
- Monthly partnership revenue: ~$25K avg
- Monthly Arroya referral: ~$3K (at 12% tier)
- 30-day settlement lag (payment received 30 days after invoice)
- **Cumulative Year 1 cash impact:** $29.5K net positive (timing-adjusted for runway modeling)

**Payment Delay Scenarios:**
If Arroya payment delayed beyond Net 30 terms:
- 30–60 day delay: Escalate to Arroya CFO; apply 1.5% late payment interest (contractual)
- >60 day delay: Escalate to CEO; consider hold on additional feature work / SLA degradation
- >90 day delay: Trigger dispute resolution; CEO approves withhold of support/reporting services

**Accrual Accounting:**
- Revenue recognized on invoice date (not payment date) per ASC 606
- Allowance for doubtful accounts = 2% of Arroya receivable (conservative)
- Monthly accounts receivable aging report reviewed in financial close

### 2.3 Currency and Tax Considerations

- All payments in USD
- Arroya responsible for any currency conversion if they remit in other currencies
- Arroya provides W-9 equivalent or equivalent tax documentation
- CULT Inc. withholding on payments subject to applicable US tax law

## 3. Marketing Development Fund (MDF) and Co-Marketing

### 3.1 MDF Allocation

**Total MDF Pool (Year 1):**
- **Amount:** $20,000 (capped)
- **Allocation:** Quarterly disbursement up to $5K per quarter
- **Funding Source:** Sourced from Arroya partnership budget (not deducted from referral fees)

### 3.2 Eligible MDF Activities

**Approved Uses:**
- Co-branded case studies (with Arroya branding and messaging)
- Joint webinars, roundtables, or industry events
- Arroya-specific solution sheets or product brief
- Content development (blog, white papers, videos) featuring Arroya integration
- Event sponsorships (booth, speaking slots) at cannabis industry trade shows
- Lead generation campaigns (digital ads, email, partnerships) mentioning Arroya solution

**NOT Eligible (Arroya covers separately):**
- General CULT product marketing or brand awareness
- R&D or product development
- Sales collateral unrelated to Arroya integration
- Internal training or team development

### 3.3 MDF Governance

**Request Process:**
1. CRO submits MDF request with scope and budget 30 days in advance
2. Arroya approves in writing (email or comment on task)
3. CULT executes; provides monthly progress updates
4. Arroya reimburses within 30 days of invoice + proof of completion

**Accountability:**
- MDF requests tracked in shared spreadsheet (access to CRO + Arroya lead)
- Quarterly reconciliation: actual spend vs. allocated budget
- Unspent MDF does NOT roll over to next year
- End of Q4: mutual review of ROI; adjust Year 2 allocation if needed

**Year 2+ Adjustment:**
- Base allocation increases to $25K if Year 1 partnership revenue >$200K
- Increases to $30K if >$400K
- Capped at $40K regardless of revenue

## 4. Year-End True-Up and Reconciliation

### 4.1 Annual Reconciliation Procedure

**Timeline:**
- **Jan 15:** CULT sends Year-End Financial Summary to Arroya
- **Jan 31:** Arroya submits any discrepancy claims or reconciliation notes
- **Feb 15:** Final true-up payment (or credit) due to Arroya

**What's Reconciled:**
1. **Revenue Verification:** Confirm all partnership customer accounts and ARR included
2. **Referral Fee Verification:** Recalculate total fees based on tiered structure; reconcile vs. monthly invoices
3. **MDF Spend Verification:** Confirm actual MDF reimbursements match approved requests
4. **Payment Discrepancies:** Identify and settle any invoicing errors, late-payment disputes, or adjustments

### 4.2 Adjustments and True-Up Logic

**Scenario: Underpayment (Arroya owes more)**
- If monthly invoices underestimated partnership revenue, recalculate true referral fee
- Arroya remits difference + accrued interest (0.5% monthly) by Feb 15
- Example: If Year 1 actual revenue was $450K (vs. invoiced $380K), recalculate tiers and settle $20K+ true-up

**Scenario: Overpayment (CULT owes credit)**
- If invoiced revenue exceeded actual recognized revenue (e.g., churn, returned customer), CULT credits to future invoices
- Credit applied dollar-for-dollar to Jan–Mar invoices of following year
- If credit remaining at Mar 31, CULT issues refund check

**Scenario: MDF Discrepancy**
- Actual MDF spend vs. allocated: settle as cash adjustment in true-up
- Unused MDF (e.g., $2K unspent) = CULT credit or Arroya forfeiture (per Section 3.3)

### 4.3 Documentation for True-Up

**CULT Provides (Jan 15):**
- Audited financial summary with monthly revenue detail (customer list, ARR, conversions)
- Copy of all monthly invoices and payment receipts
- MDF ledger (approved requests, actual spend, documentation)
- Any adjustments (churn, returns, disputes) with supporting tickets

**Arroya Reviews and Approves (by Jan 31):**
- Files any discrepancy claims in writing
- Approves final true-up calculation or requests audit (external CPA if $>50K discrepancy)

## 5. Termination and Wind-Down Terms

### 5.1 Termination Rights

**Mutual Termination for Convenience:**
- Either party may terminate with **90 days' written notice**
- Effective date = 90 days after notice
- Final referral fee period = last full month of partnership (through termination date)

**Termination for Cause (Immediate):**
- Material breach of payment or reporting obligations (both parties)
- Breach of confidentiality or data protection (both parties)
- Significant quality degradation (>3 month SLA breach by CULT; >60 day revenue reporting delay by Arroya)
- Bankruptcy or insolvency of either party

**Cure Period:**
- 15 days to cure after written notice
- If not cured, terminating party may exit without further obligation

### 5.2 Wind-Down and Final Settlement

**Upon Termination:**

| Item | Handling |
|---|---|
| **Active Customers** | Arroya retains referral rights through their last invoice/renewal; no clawback |
| **New Customers (within 30 days of notice)** | No referral fee owed if customer acquired after notice date |
| **Final Invoices** | Due 30 days after termination; CULT issues final reconciliation statement |
| **Customer Data/Reporting** | CULT provides 60-day transition period for Arroya to migrate customer info; 90-day data retention minimum |
| **Transition Services** | Optional 30-day transition support (additional $5K/month fee, if Arroya requests in writing) |

### 5.3 Clawback and Dispute Resolution

**No Clawback Provisions:**
- Arroya referral fees paid through termination date are non-refundable, except for documented overpayments (see Section 4)
- MDF spend is sunk; no refund on unused allocation

**Dispute Resolution (Escalation):**
1. **Working level:** CRO + Arroya revenue lead, 15 days to negotiate
2. **Executive:** CEO + Arroya leadership, 15 days to resolve
3. **Escalation:** External mediation (non-binding) or arbitration (if mutually agreed)
4. **Binding:** Arbitration per Delaware law if mediation fails; each party bears own costs

**Disputed amounts may be withheld** pending arbitration decision, but must be placed in escrow with neutral third party (joint agreement required).

## 8. Key Assumptions and Risks

### 8.1 Revenue Assumptions (Year 1 Base Case)

- **Starting customer count:** 1 (Arroya pilot)
- **Monthly new customers:** 2–3 (ramp: slower in Q2, acceleration Q3/Q4)
- **Average customer ARR:** $50K (mix of $25K SaaS + $25K professional services)
- **Churn rate:** 8% annually (typical for early-stage SaaS)
- **Cumulative Year 1 revenue:** $300K (conservative; cash flow model uses $400K aggressive case)

**Risk: If churn >15% or new customer acquisition <2/month, Year 1 revenue falls below $200K → affects Year 2 MDF allocation**

### 8.2 Payment and Cash Flow Risks

- **Arroya payment delay:** Risk of 30–60 day extension beyond Net 30. Mitigation: escalation path (Section 2.2)
- **Collections:** 2% allowance for doubtful accounts built into financial model
- **Currency exposure:** Minimal if Arroya pays in USD; note if international expansion planned

**Risk: If >60 day payment delays occur, adjust cash flow assumptions and escalate to CEO**

### 8.3 MDF Spend Efficiency

- **Assumption:** MDF spend generates pipeline equivalent to 1–2 new customers/quarter
- **Tracking:** Monthly ledger and Q1 2027 ROI review

**Risk: If MDF ROI is negative (spend >$5K but <1 customer acquired), Year 2 allocation reduced**

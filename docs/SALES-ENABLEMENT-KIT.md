## Buyer Personas (3 Stakeholders)

Each sale involves 3 decision-makers. Sales script must address all three.

---

### Persona 1: The Ops Manager

**Who:** 10-15 years cannabis industry ops experience. Manages daily cultivation, post-production, inventory, METRC. Direct reports: 5-10.

**Psychographics:**
- **Frustration:** Spending 20+ hours/week on spreadsheets, METRC corrections, inventory reconciliation
- **Fear:** Compliance audit finding a discrepancy that wasn't caught; losing hard-won operational trust with owner/CEO
- **Aspiration:** Fewer spreadsheets, real-time visibility, confidence in batch costing, less time firefighting

**Decision Criteria:**
1. **Will it reduce my workload?** (Primary)
2. **Will my team find it easy to use?** (Mobile, intuitive, not training-heavy)
3. **Is support responsive when something breaks?** (Reliability matters; downtime = audit risk)
4. **Will it give me better data for reporting?** (Analytics, not just transaction logging)

**Value Drivers (in order of importance):**
1. Real-time METRC sync (no end-of-day scrambling)
2. Automated batch costing (know COGS immediately)
3. Session-based workflows (matches how team works, not abstract "inventory movements")
4. Mobile app for harvest/trim sessions (staff doesn't need to come to office to log data)
5. Compliance reporting dashboard (show MOC/loss documentation in one place)

**Typical Objection:**
> "We've been using LeafLogix for POS. Why do we need another system?"

**Response (ACProC):**
- **Acknowledge:** "LeafLogix is good for retail point-of-sale. CultOps is the production layer you're missing."
- **Bridge:** "You need data flowing from harvest → post-production → batch → order → delivery. Right now you're manually transcribing from post-prod into LeafLogix. That's where errors creep in, and where you lose 20 hours/week."
- **Proof:** "With CultOps, harvest weight flows automatically to batch costing, batch costing flows to order pricing. LeafLogix integrates via API so you still do retail POS there, but now your data is clean from production forward."
- **Close:** "Want to walk through how your current trim session workflow maps to CultOps? I think you'll see where the time savings come from."

---

### Persona 2: The Owner/CEO

**Who:** Built or scaled the operation from 1 to 3+ facilities. Directly involved in P&L, financing, strategic partnerships.

**Psychographics:**
- **Frustration:** Inconsistent visibility across facilities; can't answer "what's our true unit cost?" without asking ops team; worried operations will struggle if they leave
- **Fear:** Regulatory penalty that shuts down facility; key ops person leaving and operation breaking down
- **Aspiration:** Predictable operations, scalable processes, financials clear enough to interest investors/financiers

**Decision Criteria:**
1. **Does this help us scale without hiring?** (Scaling labor headcount = diluting margins)
2. **Does this reduce compliance risk?** (Regulatory certainty > feature richness)
3. **What's the total cost of ownership?** (Platform cost + implementation + training + support)
4. **Will this help me raise capital / secure financing?** (Clean financials, predictable COGS, audit-ready systems)

**Value Drivers (in order of importance):**
1. Headcount scaling without proportional labor (grow orders/batches without hiring)
2. True unit cost visibility (break-even analysis by strain, facility, season)
3. Compliance certainty (audit-ready, event-driven trails, zero penalty risk)
4. Multi-facility dashboard (see production, inventory, orders across all locations in real-time)
5. Data portability (Supabase-backed means no vendor lock-in; if you outgrow us, data is yours)

**Typical Objection:**
> "We've been managing fine with spreadsheets and METRC. What's the ROI?"

**Response (ACProC):**
- **Acknowledge:** "Spreadsheets work at small scale. But at your size, the manual overhead is expensive."
- **Bridge:** "You're spending roughly $76,800/year in ops staff time on data entry and reconciliation. CultOps cuts that to $39,500. So the platform pays for itself plus frees up capacity for your team to focus on yield optimization, new strains, or customer relationships."
- **Proof:** "I'll model your specific scenario: 2-3 facilities, 50-100 orders/month, 20-30 batches/month. Your ops team is probably 20+ hours/week on non-value-add work. CultOps removes that. Plus you get clean unit costing, which helps with financing conversations."
- **Close:** "Can I send you a 30-day parallel run plan? You run CultOps alongside your current system, zero operational risk. After 30 days, you'll see the time savings firsthand."

---

### Persona 3: The CFO / Finance Lead

**Who:** (Emerging at $2M+ revenue). Finance background. Focused on unit economics, margin, cash flow, tax/audit.

**Psychographics:**
- **Frustration:** Cost of goods sold (COGS) unclear because batch production costs are spread wrong; can't close books quickly because inventory reconciliation takes days
- **Fear:** Tax audit finding allocation errors; misreported COGS overstating profitability
- **Aspiration:** Clean financials, fast close cycles, unit economics clear enough for board/lender reporting

**Decision Criteria:**
1. **Does this give us audit-ready financials?** (Clean cost allocation, event-driven trails)
2. **Does this speed month-end close?** (Less reconciliation, faster reporting)
3. **What's the system risk?** (Data loss, system downtime, vendor stability)
4. **Can we integrate with our accounting system?** (QuickBooks, Xero, or custom accounting tools)

**Value Drivers (in order of importance):**
1. Batch-level cost allocation (production cost tied to harvest batch, not period average)
2. Automated inventory reconciliation (flag discrepancies, reduce close-cycle time)
3. Event-driven audit trail (every transaction logged; tax/audit defensible)
4. API for accounting integration (batch cost flows automatically to accounting system)
5. SaaS stability & security (Supabase SOC2, encrypted, redundant, not on-premise risk)

**Typical Objection:**
> "How do we know our financial reports are accurate if the system is new?"

**Response (ACProC):**
- **Acknowledge:** "Financial accuracy is critical, especially for audit and lender reporting."
- **Bridge:** "CultOps uses event-driven architecture—every movement (harvest, trim, packaging, order fulfillment) creates a permanent audit entry. That's actually more defensible than spreadsheet-based allocations, which auditors flag frequently."
- **Proof:** "We'll run a 30-day parallel validation: CultOps reports vs. your current close process. You'll see the variance is typically <1%, and CultOps catches errors your spreadsheets miss (like duplicate entries or timing mismatches). Plus, close cycle shrinks from 4-5 days to 2-3 because inventory is continuously reconciled, not week-end scramble."
- **Close:** "I'll have our implementation team work with your accounting software to map batch costs into your GL. You get clean numbers and faster reporting."

## Discovery Questions by Persona

Use these in sales conversations to uncover pain, build credibility, and identify deal blockers.

### For Ops Manager

**Openner:**
1. "Walk me through your current cultivation-to-order workflow. Where do you see manual data entry creating bottlenecks?"
2. "How much time does your team spend on METRC corrections or compliance adjustments each week?"
3. "When you finish a harvest, how do you currently track the batch through post-production—trim, packaging, yield?"

**Deepeners:**
4. "Have you ever had a compliance discrepancy that caught you off-guard? How did you handle it?"
5. "If you could eliminate one daily task or report, what would it be?"
6. "Do your staff currently record data on paper, spreadsheets, or a mix?"
7. "How confident are you in your batch costing accuracy right now?" (Scale 1-10)

**Close-worthy signal:** Answers reveal 15+ hours/week in manual work, compliance anxiety, or data reconciliation pain.

---

### For Owner/CEO

**Openner:**
1. "At what revenue or facility count do you think your current operations approach will break?"
2. "What's your biggest bottleneck to scaling right now—capital, labor, or operations complexity?"
3. "If you could see real-time production data across all facilities, how would that change how you run the business?"

**Deepeners:**
4. "Have you thought about raising capital or bringing in a financial partner? What would they need to see in your financials?"
5. "What's your total current spend on operations tools (METRC, spreadsheets, accountant hours for reconciliation)?"
6. "Tell me about your top ops person. If they left tomorrow, would your operation hold together?" (Probe for key-person risk)
7. "How often do you currently review unit economics by strain or facility?"

**Close-worthy signal:** Scaling ambitions, regulatory compliance anxiety, or capital-raise plans on horizon.

---

### For CFO / Finance Lead

**Openner:**
1. "How fast can you currently close out a month? Where are the bottlenecks in your close cycle?"
2. "Walk me through how you currently allocate production costs to finished goods batches."
3. "Have you ever had a variance between your internal records and a compliance audit? How did you resolve it?"

**Deepeners:**
4. "What's your COGS as a % of revenue? How confident are you in that number?" (Fishing for under-costing or allocation errors)
5. "If you needed to quickly report unit costs by strain for a financing round, could you do it in a week?"
6. "Are you currently using any accounting integrations, or is it all manual spreadsheet export?"
7. "What's your biggest financial reporting risk right now?" (Listen for inventory/allocation concerns)

**Close-worthy signal:** Slow close cycle, costing uncertainty, or upcoming audit/financing trigger.

## Objection Handling Framework

Standard objections and ACProC (Acknowledge-Bridge-Proof-Close) responses.

| Objection | Root Cause | Bridge Response | Proof | Close |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **"BioTrackTHC is trusted by regulators"** | Legacy brand = compliance confidence | BioTrackTHC ≠ compliant; you stay compliant. CultOps audits are superior: event-driven, immutable. | Show side-by-side: BioTrackTHC batch logs vs. CultOps event trail. CultOps has no "batch created" timestamp gaps. | "Want to schedule a compliance audit demo with our DBA?" |
| **"Migration from BioTrackTHC is risky"** | Fear of parallel-run complexity, data loss | We've done 5+ migrations. 30-day parallel plan: zero operational risk. | Show migration playbook. Reference customer who switched in 3 weeks. | "Let me connect you with [Operator Name]. They went live 2 months ago." |
| **"We use LeafLogix for retail POS"** | Doesn't understand the separation of concerns | CultOps is production layer. LeafLogix is retail layer. They talk via API. | Demo: CultOps batch → LeafLogix product → POS. Data flows, no double-entry. | "Can we map your current trim workflow to show where the integration sits?" |
| **"We're using spreadsheets fine"** | Doesn't see the labor cost or compliance risk | At your scale (2+ facilities), spreadsheets = $60-80K/year in labor + audit risk. | Show ROI model for their specific size. Then ask: "How many hours/week on inventory reconciliation?" | "Let's do a 2-week trial on 1 batch. If you don't see the time savings, we're out." |
| **"CultOps is startup risk"** | Concerns about company survival, data access | Supabase backing means: cloud infrastructure, data portability, audit logs = yours. Not on-premise. | "If CultOps disappears tomorrow, your data is on Supabase (industry standard). You own it." | "Let's put a 90-day check-in in the contract. If you're not seeing value, we help you migrate." |
| **"Pricing is higher than [competitor]"** | Didn't see the value or comparing wrong tier | You compared our Growth ($18K) to their Micro ($8K). Different scale. Or: price is fixed; they add $X per facility or transaction. | Run total cost calculator: platform + implementation + training. Include their transaction fees. | "Here's the TCO comparison. You'll see we're 30% cheaper when you factor in transaction costs." |
| **"We need to check with our IT team"** | Legitimate technical gating | IT concern = SaaS security, data residency, integrations. We're SOC2, Supabase, cloud-hosted. | Offer: SOC2 cert, data residency options, API specs, integration roadmap. | "I'll schedule a call with your IT team. We'll walk through security, architecture, and integration." |

## Reference Customer Selection Criteria

For Arroya pitch deck and sales conversations, we'll need 2-3 reference customers. Selection criteria:

### Priority Tiers

**Tier 1: Case Study Customers (1-2)**
- Multi-facility ($1M-$3M revenue, 2-3 facilities)
- 30-90 days post-implementation
- Measurable outcomes: labor hours saved, METRC sync time, unit cost clarity
- Willing to do a 30-min reference call + quote for pitch deck
- Production clean enough to show real screenshots (sanitized data OK)

**Tier 2: Reference Customers (1-2 additional)**
- Same profile, different stage (60+ days in)
- Can speak to scaling benefits or multi-facility coordination
- Available for ad-hoc reference calls during sales cycle

### What To Ask During Reference Prep

Before a customer agrees to reference, validate:

1. **Business Case Clarity:** "What was your biggest pain before CultOps?" → Sanitize and quote in case study
2. **ROI Realization:** "Can you quantify the labor savings?" → Use real numbers or ranges in deck
3. **Time to Value:** "When did you start seeing benefits?" → Manage Arroya sales expectations
4. **Comfort Level:** "Are you willing to be contacted by prospects?" + "Any sensitive data we shouldn't show?"
5. **Upsell Path:** "Has your team asked about [adjacent feature]?" → Identifies expansion opportunities

### Arroya Sales Deck Integration

For the Arroya pitch deck Slide 8 (Traction & Partnership), reference customers will appear as:

- **Logo + quote** (1 sentence on impact)
- **One metric** (e.g., "20% labor cost reduction in 60 days")
- **2-3 reference call scripts** (Arroya sales team can reference-check during sales)

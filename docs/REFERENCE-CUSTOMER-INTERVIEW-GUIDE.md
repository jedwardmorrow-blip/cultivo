# Reference Customer Interview Guide

**Purpose:** Systematically extract customer wins, quantified outcomes, and testimonials for Arroya pitch deck, sales collateral, and case studies.

**Audience:** Implementation team (at post-go-live), sales team (during active sales cycle).

**Timeline:** 30-45 minute interview, 60 minutes data synthesis.

---

## Pre-Interview Preparation

### For Implementation Team (Post-Go-Live, Week 2-3)

**Selection Criteria:**
- Customer is 30+ days post-implementation (stable, has real usage)
- Operations team actively using platform daily (not just created, not dormant)
- At least one measurable outcome visible (labor savings, compliance improvement, or visibility gain)
- Customer is "net promoter" candidate (unlikely to say "it's fine but we'd switch if...")
- No sensitive data restrictions (can show anonymized screenshots or real data)

**Scheduling:**
- Email: "We'd love to get your feedback on CultOps and learn how it's working for your team. Would you have 30 minutes for a call this week?"
- Avoid: Just after go-live (customer still learning), right before harvest/audit (high stress)
- Best timing: 2-4 weeks post-live, after first full operational cycle

**Prep with Customer:**
- Send this guide (frame as "we'll ask X, Y, Z")
- Ask them to have ops manager + CFO or owner available (if possible)
- Ask them to pull together any metrics (hours saved, audit results, efficiency gains)
- Make clear: we're looking for honest feedback, not a sales pitch

## Interview Script (30-45 minutes)

### Opening (2 minutes)

**Your opening:**
"Thanks for taking the time today. We're at a stage where we're building case studies and reference materials for other operators considering CultOps. We'd love to understand:
1. How your team is actually using CultOps
2. What's working, what's been harder
3. Whether you've seen measurable impact on labor, compliance, or visibility
4. Whether you'd be willing to be a reference for other operators or let us quote you in pitch materials

This is genuine feedback—we want to know what's real and what's still a work in progress. Cool?"

### Section 1: Background & Problem (5 minutes)

**1.1 - Pre-CultOps Baseline**
- "Walk me through your operations before CultOps. How many facilities, how many employees, how much annual revenue?"
- "What was your biggest operational challenge? What kept you up at night?"
- "How were you tracking inventory, batches, METRC? What tools were you using?"

**Listen for:** Spreadsheets, manual data entry, compliance anxiety, fragmentation.

**1.2 - Time Investment & Cost**
- "How much time was your ops team spending on data entry, METRC corrections, inventory reconciliation, and reporting per week?"
- "If you had to estimate, what was the annual cost of that labor overhead?"
- [Provide context: "We typically see 60-80 hours/week for a 2-3 facility operator"]

**Listen for:** Quantifiable number (this becomes your proof point).

**1.3 - Why CultOps?**
- "What made you decide to implement CultOps? What was the trigger?"
- "Did you evaluate other options? Why did you choose us?"

**Listen for:** Pain-driven decision (not feature-driven). Competitive context.

---

### Section 2: Implementation & Adoption (5 minutes)

**2.1 - Go-Live Experience**
- "How was the implementation process? What went smoothly?"
- "What was harder than expected?"
- "Did your team have any resistance to the new system?"

**Listen for:** Friction points to surface (we can address in sales calls), adoption velocity.

**2.2 - Adoption Timeline**
- "How long until your team was comfortable using CultOps for daily operations?"
- "When did you see your first 'oh, this is actually useful' moment?"

**Listen for:** Time-to-value. 2-week adoption is good, 4-week is concerning.

**2.3 - Training & Support**
- "How much training did your team need? Did our team help?"
- "Have you needed support? How responsive have we been?"

**Listen for:** Support quality (critical for reference confidence).

---

### Section 3: Outcomes & Impact (10 minutes)

**3.1 - Labor Savings**
- "You mentioned ~[X hours/week] spent on manual work before. How much time is your team spending now?"
- "What specifically is faster or no longer needed?"

**Listen for:** Quantifiable reduction. Tie to specific tasks (METRC, inventory, reporting).

**3.2 - Compliance & Accuracy**
- "Have you noticed any impact on compliance or audit readiness?"
- "Have you had an audit since implementing CultOps? If so, how did it go?"
- "Any compliance issues or discrepancies you were worried about that have been resolved?"

**Listen for:** Audit pass, discrepancy catching, regulatory confidence.

**3.3 - Visibility & Decision-Making**
- "Can you see real-time production and inventory data now? How has that changed how you run things?"
- "Have you used the batch costing or unit economics reporting? How has that helped?"
- "As a business owner/manager, what decisions are easier now?"

**Listen for:** Specific use cases (e.g., "I can now see which strains are profitable," "I can check inventory from home").

**3.4 - Headroom & Scaling**
- "If you were to add a facility or double your order volume, do you feel like CultOps would let you grow without hiring significantly more ops staff?"
- "How confident are you that your operations will hold together if your ops manager left?"

**Listen for:** Scaling confidence. Reduced key-person risk.

---

### Section 4: Challenges & Gaps (3-5 minutes)

**4.1 - What's Not Perfect?**
- "Is there anything you wish CultOps did better or differently?"
- "Any features you expected that we haven't built?"
- "Anything about the platform that frustrated your team?"

**Listen for:** Genuine product gaps (not sales objections; we'll gather those separately). Prioritize for roadmap.

**4.2 - Recommendation**
- "On a scale of 1-10, how likely are you to recommend CultOps to another operator?"
- [If 7+] "What would get you to a 9 or 10?"
- [If 6 or below] "What would need to change for you to confidently recommend us?"

**Listen for:** NPS sentiment. Conditions for reference willingness.

---

### Section 5: Reference & Testimonial Requests (5 minutes)

**5.1 - Willingness to be a Reference**
- "Would you be willing to be a reference for other operators considering CultOps? We'd probably ask you for a 20-30 minute call when they're evaluating."

**[If yes]:**
- "Great. What's the best way to reach you when someone calls?"
- "Are there any topics or data you'd prefer we don't bring up with references?"

**[If no or hesitant]:**
- "No problem. Is there a specific concern? Something we can address?"

**5.2 - Testimonial / Quote Request**
- "Would you be willing to provide a quote or testimonial for our pitch deck and marketing materials? Something like: 'Here's what we were struggling with, and here's how CultOps fixed it.'"

**[If yes]:**
- "Perfect. I'll send you a template to make it easy. You can edit it, make it your own, or we can draft something based on our conversation."

**[If no]:**
- "Totally fine. But would you let us share anonymized results (e.g., 'One 2-facility operator saw 40% labor reduction') without naming your company?"

**5.3 - Case Study Interest**
- "We're also building case studies for our website and sales materials. Would you be interested in doing a deeper case study—where we document your problem, solution, and outcomes? We'd make sure to vet it before publishing."

**[If interested]:**
- "Fantastic. We'll draft something and send it to you for approval before anything goes public."

---

### Closing (1-2 minutes)

**Your closing:**
"This is incredibly helpful. Thank you for your honesty. As we grow, we'll keep you in the loop. And if you know other operators who might benefit from CultOps, we'd love an introduction. But no pressure.

Is there anything else you think we should know or anything you'd like to see us focus on?"

## Post-Interview: Data Synthesis (60 minutes)

### Step 1: Quantify Outcomes (15 minutes)

From the interview, extract:
- **Hours/week saved:** Before vs. after (e.g., 80 hours/week → 35 hours/week = 55% reduction)
- **Annual labor cost saved:** Hours saved × hourly cost × 52 weeks (e.g., 45 hours/week × $80/hour × 52 = $187,200)
- **Time-to-value:** Days/weeks to first measurable benefit
- **Adoption rate:** % of team actively using platform
- **NPS:** Likelihood to recommend (score)

### Step 2: Identify Key Quotes (15 minutes)

Listen for authentic, specific quotes:

**Strong quote examples:**
- "I used to spend Mondays on month-end inventory reconciliation. Now it takes 30 minutes. That's a full day back."
- "We failed an audit last year because METRC discrepancies. This year, everything synced in real-time. Audit was clean."
- "As a business owner, I can now see which strains are actually profitable. That changes pricing, product mix, everything."

**Avoid:**
- Generic corporate speak ("great tool," "really helps," "solid platform")
- Vague praise ("love it," "game changer") without specifics
- Negative context ("you guys need to fix X or we'll leave")

### Step 3: Draft Case Study Outline (30 minutes)

**Structure:**
1. **Headline:** "[Operator Name]: [X% labor reduction or specific outcome]"
2. **Company snapshot:** Revenue, facilities, employees, ops challenge
3. **Before:** Specific pain points and manual workarounds
4. **Solution:** How CultOps addressed the problem
5. **Outcomes:** Quantified results (labor savings, compliance, visibility, scaling)
6. **Quote:** Authentic testimonial from ops manager or owner
7. **Next steps:** Expansion plans or future use of CultOps

**Length:** 400-600 words (one-page PDF with logo and photo).

### Step 4: Reference Call Script Prep (optional, 30 minutes if needed)

**If customer agrees to be a reference:**

Draft 2-3 talking points for the customer to lead with:

> "When prospects call, you might say:
> 1. We were spending 80+ hours a week on manual data entry before CultOps.
> 2. Six weeks in, that dropped to 35 hours a week. That's nearly 3 FTE hours per week we got back.
> 3. For us, that meant ops could focus on cultivation optimization instead of spreadsheets.
>
> If they ask about challenges, you could mention that the first two weeks required training, but after that, adoption was smooth."

Provide customer with:
- **Prospect's role** (ops manager, owner, CFO)
- **Prospect's company size** (so customer speaks to relevant pain)
- **2-3 likely questions** customer might get asked

## Interview Recording & Compliance

**Important:** Always ask for permission to record or take detailed notes.

- **With permission:** Record video or audio. This creates an authentic testimonial video down the line.
- **Without permission:** Take detailed notes, paraphrase quotes, confirm with customer post-call.
- **Data use:** Share draft case study with customer for approval before external publication.

## FAQs for Implementation Team

**Q: When should we request an interview?**
A: Week 3-4 post-go-live. Give customer time to stabilize, but capture while the win is fresh.

**Q: What if the customer has had a rough implementation?**
A: Still interview. Understand what went wrong, what we fixed, and what they learned. Sometimes 'honest feedback' is more credible than pure praise.

**Q: What if the customer isn't willing to be a reference?**
A: Respect that. You can still ask for anonymized results or permission to feature them as "a West Coast cultivation company" without naming them.

**Q: How many reference customers do we need?**
A: 3 is good. 5 is great. We're looking for coverage across operator sizes (small 1-facility, mid 3-facility, large 5+ facility) and use cases (compliance-driven vs. scaling-driven vs. unit-economics-driven).

**Q: What if we get negative feedback?**
A: It's gold. Document it, share with product team, and iterate. Reference customers are more credible if they mention challenges we addressed.

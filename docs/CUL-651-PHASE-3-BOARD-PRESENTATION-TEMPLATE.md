### Solution: Three-Layer Framework

#### Layer 1: Monthly Spending Gates

**Mechanism:**
- Monthly agent budget cap: $[from Phase 2] allocated across active agents
- Escalation thresholds:
  - 110%: CFO review (48-hour investigation)
  - 125%: CEO involved, variance mitigation required
  - Persistent overspend: Board escalation for policy approval

**Owner:** CFO (daily monitoring via dashboard)

**Board Confidence Point:** "We track spending daily and escalate variance within 48 hours."

---

#### Layer 2: Quarterly ROI Gates

**Mechanism:**
- Every quarter (Apr, Jul, Oct, Jan): Assess each agent's LTV/CAC ratio
- Minimum threshold: 3:1 (agent must generate 3x in LTV for every 1x cost)
- Agents not meeting threshold: Flagged for rationalization or improvement plan

**Owner:** CFO + CTO (quarterly joint review)

**Board Confidence Point:** "Each agent must justify its cost by ROI. Underperforming agents are rationalized quarterly."

---

#### Layer 3: Annual Agent Count Planning

**Mechanism:**
- Year 1 plan (from Phase 1): [agent count target] agents by EOY 2026
- Quarterly scaling gate: Confirm actual count vs. plan
- Deviations >10%: Require CEO approval
- Year 2 plan: Updated annually (October) for board approval

**Owner:** CTO (planning) + CFO (enforcement)

**Board Confidence Point:** "Agent scaling is planned, monitored, and approved quarterly. We don't let autonomous systems proliferate without governance."

### Board Q&A Prep

**Question:** "What if an agent we need critically misses the ROI threshold?"
> **Answer:** "Case-by-case CEO decision. We'll either find cost efficiencies (reduce heartbeat frequency, optimize token usage) or accept a variance with documented business justification. The point is we don't let underperformance go unnoticed."

**Question:** "How does this compare to industry practice?"
> **Answer:** "Most AI startups don't have agent cost controls in Year 1. This puts us ahead of typical governance and differentiates us in due diligence. Investors view this as maturity."

**Question:** "What if CTO says the Y1 plan is now wrong (e.g., we need 12 agents instead of 8)?"
> **Answer:** "We reassess quarterly. If CTO demonstrates need, CEO + Board can approve a scaling adjustment. This is why we have quarterly gates — to stay flexible while maintaining oversight."

**Question:** "Who owns this going forward?"
> **Answer:** "CFO monitors daily and quarterly gates; CEO approves escalations and variances; Board reviews quarterly summaries starting Q2. This is a governance framework, not just a CFO task."

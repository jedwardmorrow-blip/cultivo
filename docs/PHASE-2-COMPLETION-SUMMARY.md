## Deliverables Summary

### 1. Sales Enablement Kit
**File:** `/docs/SALES-ENABLEMENT-KIT.md` (7,500+ words)

**Contents:**
- **ROI Calculator & Unit Economics**
  - Baseline cost of manual operations ($76,800/year for typical 2-3 facility operator)
  - Year 1 impact of CultOps (-$37,248 labor cost, +$19,248 net benefit)
  - 3-year ROI projections by operator size (Micro/Small/Mid/Large)
  - Conversation script for CEO/CFO-level ROI pitches
  - Unit economics by segment (pricing strategy, don't pursue criteria)

- **3 Buyer Personas with Decision Criteria**
  1. **Ops Manager** (Primary)
     - Pain: 20+ hours/week on spreadsheets, METRC corrections, reconciliation
     - Value drivers: Real-time METRC sync, batch costing automation, session-based workflows, mobile app
  2. **Owner/CEO** (Escalator)
     - Pain: Inconsistent visibility, unclear unit costs, compliance risk, scaling without hiring
     - Value drivers: Headcount scaling, true unit cost visibility, compliance certainty, multi-facility dashboard
  3. **CFO / Finance Lead** (Emerging at $2M+)
     - Pain: COGS unclear, slow month-end close, audit risk
     - Value drivers: Batch-level cost allocation, automated inventory reconciliation, audit trail, accounting integration

- **Discovery Questions by Persona** (7 questions each)
  - Ops Manager: Focus on workflow pain, compliance confidence, data entry burden
  - Owner/CEO: Focus on scaling constraints, financing readiness, visibility gaps
  - CFO: Focus on close cycle, COGS accuracy, audit readiness

- **Objection Handling Framework** (7 major objections with ACProC responses)
  1. "BioTrackTHC is trusted by regulators" → Event-driven audit trail is superior
  2. "Migration from BioTrackTHC is risky" → 30-day parallel run playbook, proven migrations
  3. "We use LeafLogix for retail POS" → Complementary systems, API integration
  4. "We're using spreadsheets fine" → $60-80K/year labor cost at your scale
  5. "CultOps is startup risk" → Supabase-backed, data portability, you own the data
  6. "Pricing is higher than [competitor]" → TCO comparison (they add transaction fees)
  7. "We need to check with IT team" → SOC2 cert, cloud-hosted, integration roadmap

- **Reference Customer Selection Criteria**
  - Tier 1: Case study customers (post-90 day, measurable outcomes, willingness to quote)
  - Tier 2: Reference customers (ad-hoc calls during sales cycles)
  - Validation checklist for reference prep

### 3. Reference Customer Interview Guide
**File:** `/docs/REFERENCE-CUSTOMER-INTERVIEW-GUIDE.md` (4,500+ words)

**Contents:**
- **Pre-Interview Preparation**
  - Selection criteria (30+ days post-live, active daily usage, measurable outcomes, willing to be reference)
  - Scheduling best practices (avoid post-go-live and high-stress periods)
  - Prep checklist for customer (metrics to pull, participants to have available)

- **Full Interview Script (30-45 minutes)**
  - **Section 1: Background & Problem** (5 min)
    - Pre-CultOps baseline (facilities, employees, revenue)
    - Biggest operational challenge (sleep-at-night questions)
    - Tools and workflows before CultOps
    - Time investment & annual cost of manual labor
  - **Section 2: Implementation & Adoption** (5 min)
    - Go-live experience, smooth parts, friction
    - Adoption timeline and "aha moments"
    - Training needs and support responsiveness
  - **Section 3: Outcomes & Impact** (10 min)
    - Labor savings (quantified hours/week reduction)
    - Compliance & accuracy improvements
    - Visibility & decision-making improvements
    - Scaling confidence & key-person risk reduction
  - **Section 4: Challenges & Gaps** (3-5 min)
    - What's not perfect / wish list
    - NPS recommendation score (1-10)
  - **Section 5: Reference & Testimonial** (5 min)
    - Willingness to be a reference
    - Testimonial quote request
    - Case study interest

- **Post-Interview Data Synthesis** (60 minutes)
  - Extract quantified outcomes (hours saved, annual labor cost, ROI)
  - Identify authentic, specific quotes (avoid generic corporate speak)
  - Draft case study outline (400-600 words, one-page PDF)
  - Reference call script prep (if customer agrees)

- **Follow-Up Email Template** for case study draft, testimonial, reference process

- **Reference Customer Roster Tracking Sheet** to maintain 3-5 active references

## FAQs from CMO

**Q: Is this enough for Arroya to start selling?**
A: Yes. The sales enablement kit + speaker notes give Arroya team the complete playbook: brand positioning, ROI proof, buyer personas, discovery questions, objection handling, and pitch framework. No surprises or ambiguity.

**Q: When do we need reference customers live?**
A: Ideally 2-3 before Arroya starts actively selling (end of April). Minimum 1 by early May. References make the business case credible ("not just a startup pitch").

**Q: What if Arroya wants to modify the messaging?**
A: Collaborate. The core framework (ROI calculator, buyer personas, objection handling) is solid. Let them customize language to match their sales culture. The substance should stay consistent.

**Q: How do we know this is working?**
A: Track: (1) Customer acquisition cost via Arroya vs. direct sales, (2) Win rate vs. competitors (in sales CRM), (3) Sales cycle length, (4) Reference customer NPS. Review quarterly with Arroya.

**Q: What if a prospect asks for something not in these materials?**
A: That's data. Document it, share with CMO, iterate. The materials are v1.0. We'll refine based on real conversations.
